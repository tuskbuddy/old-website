SPANISH CHICKEN & RICE


This one is a family favorite bachelor favorite. You can sub in a 1/2 cup of diced green peppers if you're not a fan of olives. I'm not normally big on olives, but I find they work well with the dish. If you can eat olives on pizza, you may want to use them; if you don't, there's no shame in that. Take them out or sub them out with confidence.


MOROCCAN CHICKEN THIGHS


Skip cooking the chicken in the skillet. Instead, cut the chicken lengthwise and put it and the other ingredients in the oven from the get-go. It's quicker, easier, and there's more of a chance the chicken won't be death-valley dry.
Sub-tips: Make sure you have carrots on hand. The cayenne pepper isn't necessary for this recipe. You can sub in zucchini and summer squash for mission figs and green olives.


SKINNY BURRITO BOWL


Full disclaimer: I have this recipe on my site to remind me of my own recipe for taco day. I like tacos, and I need the reminder. Tacos are easy, and lovely.
I made this recipe a long time ago and took out the yogurt and beans but now I just make my own tacos with taco-seasoned chicken, diced bell peppers, rice, corn, romaine lettuce, taco shells, lime juice, and guacamole. Mmm.


LIME CHICKEN FAJITAS


I like to use chopped chicken for this recipe, and 1.5 to 2 lb of chicken is usually good for the called-for amount of marinade. You can also add 1/8 tsp red pepper for some spice.




BBQ PULLED CHICKEN


A slow cooker recipe ideal for if you have 1 hr of prep time in the morning but no time before dinner. If you're a bachelor(ette) like me, you may want to cut the recipe in half. The recipe also makes a lot of liquid, so don't be afraid to add a little more tomato paste.




CAULIFLOWER RICE SKILLET


If you're looking for a new, mildly interesting recipe, this might be the recipe for you. The cauliflower rice isn't going to dazzle you but it may help you sneak in some veggies into your system, which is always a plus.

This recipe is best with 2 lb of chicken and double the taco seasoning. I'm not a big fan of beans, and I like to sub in 2 bell peppers in its place.




COCKTAIL MEATBALLS


Unless you want to use some of the meatball sauce for dipping, serving, or bathing, use 2 lb of ground beef. You can also reduce the sauce measurements to 75% with 1.5 lb beef, which I often do given that many cranberry sauce cans only carry 1.5 cups.

No need to use red pepper flakes, since the sweet chili sauce will likely be spicy enough. Do note that the spiciness of sweet chili sauce varies by brand: you should be getting a mild or medium level sauce. Maggi brand and Meijer brand are ok; Walmart brand is not ok. Sweet chili sauce can be found in aisles with ethnic food or dipping sauce for wings.




CHICKEN FRIED RICE


I love fried rice.

Use less sesame oil than it calls for. Add some lemon juice to offset the bitter flavor if you've added too much. This works best with days-old rice, so make a several-cup batch of rice a few days before. I like to add more adult chicken to replace the baby chicken. (1.5 - 2 lb chicken, omit eggs.) I also like using broccoli, bell peppers, or more carrots to replace the peas and green onions.




HONEY GARLIC CHICKEN


Two chicken breasts comes to about 1 lb of chicken, so scale accordingly. The sweet potato, honey, and oregano are great together. A bit oily.




GARLIC CHICKEN AND POTATOES


I'm a complete sucker for sauces with brown sugar.
When the potatoes & chicken are in together, don't cook them for more than 17 minutes, since the chicken went dry after only 20. 15 minutes is likely the sweet spot, but I need more data to make sure that there are no renegade bacteria in the chicken at that temperature.




ROSEMARY CHICKEN MARINADE


The author calls this recipe 'the best chicken marinade', and it's certainly a contender. You can either grill the chicken or bake it for this recipe. Since I don't have a family of my own, a wooden patio, or a sixth sense for when the burgers are done, I like to bake the chicken. I also like to cut the recipe in half if I'm serving 1-2 people.




TURMERIC CHICKEN SKILLET


This is the yellowest meal I've ever made. If you're cooking this for 1-2 people, use a whole head of cabbage and don't bother with the spinach or kale. You can also get away with using ground cilantro instead of the fresh stuff.




GARLIC BASIL CHICKEN AND PASTA


Note to self: I tried it using canned diced tomatoes and I used more pasta and more chicken and lots of butter and it was okay. Be a law-abiding citizen and follow the recipe if you make this again.




CAJUN CHICKEN AND SAUSAGE


An unspoken theme of this recipe book of a website is chicken. Over 90% of my main dish recipes involve chicken. It's just so good. I'm quite aware of this, and this recipe is a meek attempt at mixing up my meat repertoire.

The first time I made this, I did not have diced tomatoes on hand and it still turned out well. 12 oz of pasta was a little too much - use 9-10 oz of pasta next time with roughly the same amount of chicken broth. Or add more chicken broth for the same amount of pasta. I also needed 15-20 minutes on med-high heat to boil the pasta, but I used rigatoni and the thickness of the noodles may have been a factor. Cream cheese and scallions not necessary.




THAI BASIL CHICKEN BOWL


Tip: don't be like me and go to the supermarket and take over a minute to open a plastic bag then see a couple who was hanging around you and grab two jalapeños when you finally get the bag open because you didn't want to be that guy who took forever to open the bag just to get one jalapeño. And don't think "Ah why don't I just use both jalapeños in this recipe" because more than one jalapeño is definitely too spicy.

That said, this is a solid stir-fry recipe. The original cooks do use some peculiar ingredients cause they follow a paleo diet, but you can make the following subs: olive oil for avocado oil, soy sauce for coconut aminos, Worcestershire sauce for fish sauce, and cornstarch for arrowroot powder. And go easy on the jalapeño.




ULTIMATE CHICKEN NOODLE SOUP


Excellent as is. I like to sub in rice noodles or cooked rice in for the egg noodles. If you're cooking for one, cut the recipe in half.




CHICKEN TORTILLA SOUP


Don't use the jalapeño. Do use 1/2 tsp chili powder and a whole green pepper. Cooking for one: use 3/4 of the original recipe, because you don't want to eat soup for breakfast, lunch, and dinner unless you're a leftovers connoisseur.




PAD THAI


Hands-down the best pad thai recipe I've ever made. It is the only pad thai recipe I've ever made, but it's far too good to let any other recipe even attempt to claim superiority to this restaurant-grade meal.

Personal notes: I used 8 oz brown rice noodles; 1 lb chicken; 2 tbsp rice vinegar with 0.5 tbsp ketchup; 2 tbsp worcestershire sauce for every 3 tbsp fish sauce; used sunflower butter for peanut butter; and doubled the sauce.




TACOS DE BARBACOA


The recipe says to cut the meat into four pieces, but I found that eight pieces was more reasonable to work with. For anything less than a family of four, cut this recipe in half if possible. 4 pounds of barbacoa is a lot of meat. It's also expensive and takes a long time to braise.

That said, I made the full recipe for myself, had 3.5 pounds of leftovers, and ate all the leftovers in less than a week. So delicious. Mostly because barbacoa meat is so juicy when it's braised right.




CHICKEN & PASTA WITH SALSA VERDE


Use 1/2 lb penne, 1.5 lb chicken, a little more cheese, and start with 1/2 c salsa verde - it depends on the brand, but some variations of salsa verde are spicier than my newest mixtape. Go by taste for the salsa verde and work your way up to 3/4 cup or 1 cup max.




CHICKEN ENCHILADAS


Haahhhhhhhhh, sooo good! I believe I used 1 cup cream cheese and 1.5 c colby jack in place of the sour cream and cheese. I made this recipe before I cut down on using gluten and dairy products, so I have no way of knowing if the original recipe or my varied recipe is better - please give me feedback on this one!




GARLIC HERB SPELT BREADSTICKS


Mmm, breadsticks. Best made with white spelt flour, but wheat flour makes good breadsticks too, especially when warm and paired with heaps of marinara sauce. The recipe calls for 3 cups of spelt flour, but you'll likely need 4. Makes 12-18 breadsticks. Make small breadsticks to start and spread the baking sheet with olive oil before letting rise.




ZUCCHINI CHOCOLATE CHIP MUFFINS


I haven't tried this recipe but I can't find the zucchini bread recipe I normally use. I had to include this because zucchini bread is just so so good.

Personal notes: use spelt flour and oat milk instead of wheat flour and skim milk.




CHOCOLATE CHIP COOKIES


This is the only chocolate chip cookie I will ever make. A little crisp on the outside, chewy on the inside, and it's a hit everywhere I take it.

There are two important things to know about this recipe:
1) Use 1.5 cups of chocolate chips! This lady has some mad chocolate chip fever because she beckons you to use 50% more chocolate chips. Don't do it! It's not worth the sweet, sweet pain! You'll be drowning in chocolate chip sorrow before you realize your terrible mistake.
2) Apart from the chocolate chip malarky, do follow the instructions to a tee. Don't mix the dough any more than absolutely necessary, and set the butter out a little early (or microwave for 8 seconds) to get it to room temperature. The cookies should seem very nearly done when you take them out of the oven and then firm up nicely after cooling.

I've made variations of this where I used entirely white sugar. I also like to sprinkle a pinch of cinnamon and a pinch of a pinch of nutmeg on the top of each cookie before baking.




SPELT BANANA BREAD


Excellent. You can use entirely spelt flour for this recipe.

Be sure to cover the pan with aluminum foil when baking - I needed to cook it at 300 for 30 more minutes to get it to solidify in the center.
